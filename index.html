ABOUT ME

I graduated from the Urban and Rural Planning Discipline at Khulna University, where I built my focus around GIS and Remote Sensing. My work sits at the meeting point of spatial data science and social science: multi-hazard mapping, susceptibility modelling, and vulnerability assessment.

I integrate satellite-based spatial data with GIS, machine learning, and deep learning tools to visualize how change plays out over land and the people living on it. The goal is always the same, give planners and policymakers something concrete to act on.

I have worked across cyclone, flood, landslide, and earthquake hazard problems in Bangladesh, and I completed an internship at SPARRSO working on Kaptai Lake boundary delineation with Sentinel-1 and Sentinel-2 imagery.

Q1 · Published

Landslide Susceptibility & Road Vulnerability

Ensemble deep learning framework mapping landslide-prone zones and road vulnerability in the Chittagong Hill Tracts.

Objective: Predict landslide-prone zones and rank roads by exposure to slope failure across the hill region.

Findings: The ensemble of DNN, CNN and LSTM outperformed the individual models, and the road vulnerability index flagged the most exposed transport links for priority action.

Undergrad Thesis

Flood Susceptibility in the Teesta Basin

SAR data and ensemble machine learning to model flash-flood susceptibility across the transboundary Teesta River basin.

Objective: Map flood susceptibility before and after upstream dam openings and trace the shortest evacuation routes.

Findings: Susceptibility rose sharply after dam release, and the ensemble model gave the most reliable map when checked against community agreement.

Submitted

Cyclone Vulnerability, Chittagong City

GIS-based MCDA with Analytic Hierarchy Process to assess cyclone vulnerability in Chittagong City Corporation.

Objective: Combine physical and social layers into one cyclone vulnerability picture for the city corporation.

Findings: Low-elevation coastal wards near the cyclone track carried the highest vulnerability, pointing to where shelter capacity needs to grow.

Remote Sensing

Seasonal Surface Water Extent

Tracking seasonal variation in water extent across pre-monsoon, monsoon, and post-monsoon periods using satellite data.

Objective: Measure how surface water spreads and recedes through the pre-monsoon, monsoon and post-monsoon seasons.

Findings: Water extent peaked in the monsoon and shrank markedly by the post-monsoon period, giving a clear read on flood timing and water availability.
